What is Neighbourhood Signals?

Neighbourhood Signals is a local listening layer. It helps us hear how a place is trying to navigate support, before pressure hardens into crisis.

It listens at a place level, not a person level. It does not track individuals, create profiles, or behave like a clinical system. It is not medical triage. It is neighbourhood intelligence.

  • What people are trying to find
  • Which doors they are trying
  • Where uncertainty is growing
  • Where shared need is beginning to form pressure

Why this matters

By the time pressure shows up in activity data, appointments, or crisis services, it has already been felt.
People have been coping, adapting, and searching for a way through.

Signals gives us something rare, time. Time to notice earlier, to adapt together, and to shape responses while needs are still light and preventable.

The Local Social, the access layer that enables Signals

Neighbourhood Signals only works if people have somewhere simple and trusted to start. That is what The Local Social provides. It’s the shared door, online and in the real world.

  • A calm, quick door into local support
  • A shared starting point across partners
  • A neighbourhood-shaped access layer, because people can move the doors to where they think they belong

When people use these doors, we learn something place-level and practical, what the neighbourhood is trying to do.
That shared access layer becomes the listening surface for Neighbourhood Signals.

Visit thelocalsocial.org

Listening without surveillance

The intent is simple. We don’t watch people. We listen to the neighbourhood.

Signals is about patterns and shifts, not individuals. It is designed to be trusted, and to stay ethically clean.

What this gives partners

Used well, Signals helps partners see emerging pressure before it tips, understand where navigation is breaking down, and shape responses earlier.

  • Earlier awareness of rising need
  • Better shared understanding across NHS, PCN, Council, voluntary sector, and advice services
  • More timely demand shaping, communications, and pathway adjustments
  • A calmer, more preventative posture as a system
